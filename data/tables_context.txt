# Company Database — Table Documentation

## Table: employees
Description: Stores employee records for the organization.
Columns:
  - employee_id (INTEGER, PRIMARY KEY): Unique identifier for each employee.
  - full_name (TEXT): Employee's full name.
  - department (TEXT): Department name (Engineering, Sales, HR, Finance).
  - hire_date (DATE): Date the employee was hired.
  - salary (DECIMAL): Annual salary in USD.

Sample rows:
  | employee_id | full_name      | department  | hire_date  | salary  |
  |-------------|----------------|-------------|------------|---------|
  | 1           | Alice Johnson  | Engineering | 2021-03-15 | 95000   |
  | 2           | Bob Smith      | Sales       | 2019-07-01 | 72000   |
  | 3           | Carol Lee      | HR          | 2020-11-20 | 68000   |
  | 4           | David Kim      | Engineering | 2022-01-10 | 102000  |

---

## Table: products
Description: Product catalog for the e-commerce platform.
Columns:
  - product_id (INTEGER, PRIMARY KEY): Unique product identifier.
  - product_name (TEXT): Display name of the product.
  - category (TEXT): Product category (Electronics, Furniture, Accessories).
  - unit_price (DECIMAL): Price per unit in USD.
  - stock_quantity (INTEGER): Units available in warehouse.

Sample rows:
  | product_id | product_name    | category    | unit_price | stock_quantity |
  |------------|-----------------|-------------|------------|----------------|
  | 101        | Wireless Mouse  | Electronics | 29.99      | 450            |
  | 102        | Office Chair    | Furniture   | 249.00     | 85             |
  | 103        | USB-C Hub       | Electronics | 45.50      | 320            |
  | 104        | Desk Lamp       | Accessories | 35.00      | 200            |

---

## Table: orders
Description: Customer purchase orders linking products to sales totals.
Columns:
  - order_id (INTEGER, PRIMARY KEY): Unique order identifier.
  - customer_name (TEXT): Name of the customer who placed the order.
  - product_id (INTEGER, FOREIGN KEY -> products.product_id): Product purchased.
  - quantity (INTEGER): Number of units ordered.
  - order_date (DATE): Date the order was placed.
  - total_amount (DECIMAL): Total price (quantity * unit_price at time of order).

Sample rows:
  | order_id | customer_name | product_id | quantity | order_date | total_amount |
  |----------|---------------|------------|----------|------------|--------------|
  | 5001     | Jane Doe      | 101        | 2        | 2025-06-01 | 59.98        |
  | 5002     | John Roe      | 102        | 1        | 2025-06-03 | 249.00       |
  | 5003     | Jane Doe      | 103        | 1        | 2025-06-10 | 45.50        |
  | 5004     | Acme Corp     | 101        | 50       | 2025-06-15 | 1499.50      |

---

## Table: department_budgets
Description: Annual budget allocation per department.
Columns:
  - department (TEXT, PRIMARY KEY): Department name.
  - fiscal_year (INTEGER): Budget year.
  - allocated_budget (DECIMAL): Total budget in USD.
  - spent_to_date (DECIMAL): Amount spent so far in USD.

Sample rows:
  | department  | fiscal_year | allocated_budget | spent_to_date |
  |-------------|-------------|------------------|---------------|
  | Engineering | 2025        | 1200000          | 780000        |
  | Sales       | 2025        | 450000           | 310000        |
  | HR          | 2025        | 200000           | 145000        |
  | Finance     | 2025        | 180000           | 92000         |

---

## Relationships
- orders.product_id references products.product_id
- employees.department can be joined with department_budgets.department for budget vs. payroll analysis

## Business rules
- Engineering has the highest average salary among departments.
- Bulk orders (quantity >= 50) receive an implicit 10% discount reflected in total_amount.
- Products with stock_quantity below 100 are considered low stock and should be reordered.
